02

The Discovery

Most reps treat discovery as the preamble to the pitch. It is the pitch.

STEP 01

Surface

Open the door. Let them talk.

"Tell me more about that."

STEP 02

Impact

Connect problem to consequence.

"What's the cost of this problem for you?"

STEP 03

Stakes

Reveal urgency they feel — don't manufacture it.

"What happens if this doesn't get solved?"

The rep who asks better questions wins.

THE GREATEST LINE IN SALES

"How will it impact your business if X doesn't happen?"

After you've found the problem — this is the only question that matters.

04

The Pitch

A great pitch is a mirror. It reflects back what you learned in discovery.

Heard

"Here's what I heard..."

Prove you listened. Repeat their words back to them. They need to feel understood before they'll trust your solution.

Matters

"Here's why that matters..."

Connect their problem to the business impact. Make the stakes real. This is where urgency comes from.

Solve

"Here's exactly how we solve it."

Now — and only now — you present the solution. Mapped directly to what they told you. No generic demo.

It's not a deck. It's a conversation you already had — played back with precision.

06

Pipeline

Activity ≠ Progress.

Always have a next step

Every meeting ends with the next meeting scheduled. No exceptions. Vague follow-ups are deals dying slowly.

Single-threaded deals are dead deals

One contact = one veto. Get multi-threaded early. Your champion needs an ally.

Movement = their action, not yours

Sending emails is not pipeline movement. The deal moves when they do something.

Audit your pipeline weekly

If you can't say what changed in the last 7 days, the deal isn't real.

The Science vs. The Art

The Science

Sets the floor. Without it, you're guessing.

  • Qualification frameworks — MEDDIC, BANT, SPIN
  • Pipeline math — coverage ratios, conversion by stage
  • CRM discipline — if it's not in the system, it didn't happen
  • Cadence & sequencing — when, how, and how often
  • Forecasting — data over gut feel
  • Repeatable process — so it works no matter who runs it

The Art

Sets the ceiling. This is where top performers separate.

  • Emotional intelligence — reading what's not being said
  • Trust-building — 53% of B2B loyalty is the sales experience itself
  • Storytelling — 102% greater persuasion through narrative
  • Tone, timing, intuition — knowing when to push, when to pause
  • Empathy — buyers are 50% more likely to buy when they feel understood
  • Knowing when to shut up and listen
